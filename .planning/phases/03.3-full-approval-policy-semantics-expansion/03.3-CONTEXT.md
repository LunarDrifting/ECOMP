# Phase 3.3: Full Approval Policy Semantics Expansion - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-02-28
**Status:** Ready for planning
**Source:** User-provided phase goal and constraints

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Expand approval policy enforcement in `markTaskDone` from minimal checks to deterministic policy-specific semantics for `SINGLE`, `SEQUENTIAL`, `PARALLEL`, and `QUORUM`.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Locked Decisions
- No schema changes.
- Service-layer only.
- Must follow `docs/ARCHITECTURE_CONTRACT.md`.
- Must enforce tenant isolation strictly.
- Must use `tenantDb` wrapper only.
- Must not modify projection layer.
- Must not introduce background workers.
- Must preserve idempotency.
- Sequential execution only.

### Required Policy Semantics
1. `NONE`
   - No approval required.
2. `SINGLE`
   - Require at least one `APPROVED`.
3. `SEQUENTIAL`
   - Reject if any `REJECTED` exists.
   - Otherwise require at least one `APPROVED`.
   - No role sequencing in this phase.
4. `PARALLEL`
   - Minimal interpretation only.
   - Require at least one `APPROVED` approval from a user whose `roleId` equals `task.ownerRoleId`.
   - No required-role matrix, no distinct-role counting, no dynamic inference.
5. `QUORUM`
   - Require at least `N=2` `APPROVED` approvals from distinct `actorId` values.

### Behavior Requirements
- Load tenant-scoped approval rows once per `markTaskDone` call (no N+1).
- Load actor roleIds once per `markTaskDone` call (batched, tenant-scoped).
- Evaluate approval policy deterministically.
- Canonical failure message: `Approval policy requirements not satisfied`.
- Map policy failure to HTTP 409.
- No mutation and no resolver call on policy failure.
- Preserve existing behavior:
  - BLOCKED rejection
  - already-DONE idempotency
  - PRECONDITION gate enforcement ordering
  - resolver invocation only after successful DONE mutation

### Claude's Discretion
- Internal helper decomposition for policy evaluation.
- Exact tenantDb read helper shapes for minimal PARALLEL evaluation as long as reads are tenant-scoped and batched.

</decisions>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- Introduce deterministic policy evaluator function in service.
- Use one approval load and one actor-roleId load for policy evaluation.
- Keep route thin; only adjust error mapping if canonical message handling is required.

</specifics>

<deferred>
## Deferred Ideas

- Dynamic quorum thresholds.
- Role sequencing and weighted approvals.
- Policy-specific rejection reason surface area.
- Multi-role PARALLEL coverage semantics.

</deferred>
